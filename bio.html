Janeth Jepkogei is a member of the class of 2017 studying computer science. She was born and raised in Eldoret, Kenya. Coming to Tufts was her first time in America. At a young age, she developed an interested in traveling and because of that she desired to study abroad. Her dream of studying abroad was made possible when she joined KenSAP. KenSAP is college prep program that helps students from rural Kenya get into schools in the US.

Janeth is passionate about employing her academic skills to empower women in her community. Growing up in rural Kenya, Janeth was always aware of the challenges that women faced and the potential that women had in changing society for the better. She believes that empowering women is one way of bringing positive social impact. It is because of her desire to empower women that she spent some time after high school helping other girls in her village by tutoring them. She would set up time to teach math and sciences to the girls. She hopes that with her Tufts education she would be able to start a girls mentoring program in Kenya that supports and encourages girls to explore, discover and pursue their passions.

Apart from her academics and being a Synaptics Scholar, Janeth is also involved in the Tufts African Students Association, Tufts Film Series, Tufts Women in Computer Science and Tufts International Center. She enjoys hiking, kayaking and cooking Kenyan dishes.
